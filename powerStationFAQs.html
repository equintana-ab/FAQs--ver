Frequently Asked Questions

Here are quick answers to questions shoppers ask the most.

A power station provides reliable backup power during brownouts or emergencies. It can power multiple devices and appliances such as fans, TVs, routers, and laptops, making it ideal for home use or outdoor trips.

  • Reliable backup power during brownouts or emergencies
  • Can power multiple devices, including appliances
  • Reliable, high‑capacity battery for longer runtime
  • Stable output to protect sensitive electronics

The capacity you need depends on the devices you want to power and how long you want them to run.

Quick Guide:

Light Use (300–500Wh): Phones, WiFi, lights – perfect for short brownouts or quick backup (3–8 hours).

Moderate Use (500–1000Wh): Laptop, fan, TV – ideal for work-from-home or longer backup (4–10 hours).

Heavy Use (1000–2000Wh+): Fridge, multiple devices – great for whole-room or whole-home backup (6–20 hours).

High-power appliances like induction stoves drain batteries very fast. For these, look for:

  • Battery capacity 1500Wh+
  • Inverter output 2000W+
Simple rule:
  • Backup only → 500Wh
  • Work + comfort → 1000Wh
  • Whole-home backup → 1500Wh+

Tip: Always choose a power station with a slightly higher output than your total load and leave 20–30% extra capacity as a buffer for safety and longer runtime.

To choose the right one:

  • List the devices you want to power (e.g., fan, TV, laptop).
  • Check their wattage and add them up to know your power requirement.
  • Choose a power station with a higher capacity and output than your total needs.
  • Consider battery life, output ports, charging options, and portability to match how and where you’ll use it.
